Privacy Policy

Last Updated: October 15, 2024

Introduction

BrainSAIT Technologies ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal and health information. This Privacy Policy explains how AFHAM (أفهم) collects, uses, processes, and protects your information in compliance with the Saudi Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL) and applicable healthcare regulations.

Information We Collect

Personal Information

  • Account information (name, email address, professional credentials)
  • Healthcare facility information and institutional affiliations
  • Professional licensing information for healthcare providers
  • Communication preferences and language settings

Health Information (PHI)

  • Medical documents uploaded for processing and analysis
  • Extracted text and structured data from medical records
  • AI-generated insights and analysis results
  • Voice recordings for transcription services (when enabled)

Technical Information

  • Device information and operating system details
  • Application usage statistics and performance metrics
  • Network information and IP addresses (anonymized)
  • Error logs and crash reports (without PHI)

How We Use Your Information

Primary Purposes

  • Document processing and AI-powered analysis for healthcare workflows
  • Providing intelligent chat responses and clinical decision support
  • Voice-to-text transcription for medical documentation
  • NPHIES compliance validation and FHIR resource generation

Secondary Purposes (with explicit consent)

  • Product improvement and feature development
  • Anonymized research for advancing healthcare AI
  • Quality assurance and accuracy improvements
  • Technical support and customer service

PDPL Compliance

Lawful Basis for Processing

  • Explicit consent for health information processing
  • Legitimate interests for service provision and improvement
  • Compliance with healthcare regulations and NPHIES requirements
  • Contractual necessity for healthcare service delivery

Your Rights Under PDPL

  • Right to access your personal and health information
  • Right to rectification of inaccurate information
  • Right to erasure (with healthcare record retention requirements)
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability in standard healthcare formats
  • Right to object to processing
  • Right to withdraw consent at any time

Data Security

Technical Safeguards

  • AES-256 encryption for data at rest and in transit
  • Multi-factor authentication for healthcare professionals
  • Role-based access control with audit trails
  • Regular security assessments and penetration testing
  • Secure API endpoints with rate limiting and monitoring

Administrative Safeguards

  • Employee training on healthcare data privacy
  • Incident response procedures for data breaches
  • Regular compliance audits and assessments
  • Vendor management and third-party security requirements

Data Sharing and Disclosure

We Do Not Sell Personal Information

AFHAM does not sell, rent, or trade your personal or health information to third parties for commercial purposes.

Limited Sharing Scenarios

  • Healthcare providers within your institution (with proper authorization)
  • NPHIES platform for compliance and healthcare data exchange
  • Cloud service providers with strict data processing agreements
  • Legal authorities when required by law or court order
  • Emergency situations to protect patient safety

Data Retention

  • Personal account information: Retained while account is active
  • Health information: Retained according to healthcare regulations (minimum 7 years)
  • Technical logs: Retained for 1 year for security purposes
  • Anonymized research data: Retained indefinitely for healthcare advancement

International Data Transfers

AFHAM primarily processes data within Saudi Arabia. When international transfers are necessary for service provision, we ensure:

  • Adequate protection measures equivalent to PDPL standards
  • Data processing agreements with international processors
  • Explicit consent for cross-border health data transfers
